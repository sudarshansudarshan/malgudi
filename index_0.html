Research

My research interests include social networks, crowdscoured knowledge building and computational social sciences. I enjoy working on problems that interest me. My current interests are mostly steered by my PhD students, which include "Predicting a Viral meme" (Yayati Gupta), "Understanding Crowdsourced Knowledge buidling" (Anamika Chhabra), "Dissecting Bollywood's hidden formulae" (Varsha Bhat) and "Centrality Measures" (Rishi Ranjan Singh).

Teaching

 After research, teaching makes the major component of my academic life. I enjoy experimenting with different teaching methodologies. I particularly enjoy traveling and giving talks on my research work apart from motivational talks of popsci genre. For more details on my talks, check my blog.

In Spring 2015 (Jan 2015 - April 2015), I am teaching Discrete Matheamtical Structures The course web site can be accessed here: CSL 105 - Discrete Mathematical Structures .
